Art, Faith, and Healing: The Tattooist Touching Lives in Jerusalem

By Isabel Kershner

Tattooing has long been a practice tied to pilgrimage and religious devotion in Jerusalem, with some dating the tradition back centuries. But today, it’s not just pilgrims—artists like Isaac, a local tattooist, are finding ways to turn faith into body art that represents both belief and survival.

Isaac’s clients range from religious devotees to those scarred by conflict, seeking a way to heal through body art. His studio has become a hub for both personal expression and spiritual connection.

“The ink tells a story—a story of faith, identity, and healing.”

For those who have lived through the violence and turmoil of Jerusalem’s conflicts, tattoos can offer a form of recovery, marking not just their survival but their hope for the future.
